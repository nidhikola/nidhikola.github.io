Why Your Clothes Matter for Non-Toxic Living

What you wear touches your skin for hours every day — and it matters more than you think.

By NonToxicLife · Published January 10, 2026

When people think about non-toxic living, they usually focus on food or water. Clothes rarely come up — even though they stay in contact with our skin all day.

Conventional clothing production uses pesticides, synthetic dyes, wrinkle-resistant finishes, and chemicals like formaldehyde that do not fully wash out.

Skin is not just a barrier. Repeated exposure to certain chemicals can lead to irritation, allergic reactions, and inflammation over time.

Better Clothing Swaps

Start with items closest to your skin: underwear, pajamas, t-shirts, and leggings.

What to Look For

  • Organic cotton, linen, hemp, or wool
  • GOTS or OEKO-TEX certifications
  • Minimal finishes and untreated fabrics
