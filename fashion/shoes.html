Shoes

More Than Just Comfort and Style

By NonToxicLife · Published January 10, 2026

Shoes are something most people wear for hours every day, yet very few people think about what they are made of. Most shoes today are built using synthetic rubber, plastic foams, dyes, and chemical adhesives. These materials are not always tested for long term skin contact, even though your feet are warm, sweaty, and enclosed for most of the day.

Heat and moisture matter here. When feet sweat, the skin becomes more permeable, which means chemicals from dyes, glues, and synthetic materials can interact more easily with the skin. Research on consumer materials shows that long term exposure to certain synthetic compounds can contribute to irritation and inflammation, especially in enclosed environments like shoes. Conventional clothing production uses pesticides, synthetic dyes, wrinkle-resistant finishes, and chemicals like formaldehyde that do not fully wash out.

Another issue is breathability. Shoes that trap moisture can increase skin irritation and odor, which often leads companies to add more chemical treatments. That creates a cycle where more products are added to fix problems caused by synthetic materials in the first place.

Better Shoe Choices

No shoe is completely non toxic, but simpler construction helps.

Better options

  • Crocs, which are made from a single molded material and avoid layered glues
  • Shoes made with natural leather, cork, or wool linings
